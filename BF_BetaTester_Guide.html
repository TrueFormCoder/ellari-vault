⚠ Safety Insert — Read First

This is a symbolic reflection and pattern-recognition test. It does not tell the future, diagnose people, prove motives, replace professional advice, or authorize escalation. Use it to clarify your own perception, boundaries, choices, and next action.

Stop immediately if you become frantic, punitive, obsessive, or more certain without new evidence.

No-read condition
Wanting punishment, prediction, certainty about another person, or permission to escalate.
Stop signal
Frantic body state, compulsive rereading, urge to pull more cards, or a stronger story without stronger evidence.
Grounding close
Name one fact. Name one ordinary next step. Put the deck away.

What counts as valid data

A stopped test is usable data. Confusion is revision signal. You do not need to complete the full session for the feedback to count.

03
20-minute session
0–2 min
Orient. Read the safety insert. Confirm this is a low-risk scenario. If not, stop here — that is valid data.
3–5 min
Ground the situation. Write one observable fact about what is happening. Not an interpretation. A fact you could photograph or point to.
6–8 min
Select one card. No explanation from the facilitator. You choose the card that seems to fit. Read it on your own first.
9–12 min
Answer the card question. One sentence. If you cannot answer in one sentence, note that — it is revision data about the card.
13–16 min
Name one small move and a done-for-now state. What will you do? When will this be complete enough to set down?
17–20 min
Score the card. Use the six scoring criteria below. Record your scores and any confusion points.

Done-State
Scale 1–5 · Target: 4+
Can you say what is complete enough for now? 1 = no clear stopping point. 5 = the stop is obvious.

Pass Standard

A card passes the first beta check when average Clarity, Safety, Actionability, Portability, and Done-State are 4 or higher, and Cognitive Load is 3 or lower. No tester reports diagnosis, prediction, motive-proof, or escalation misuse.

07
Facilitator script
Opening
"Today we are testing whether one card can help you move from confusion to one clean next step. This is not therapy, legal advice, diagnosis, prediction, or crisis support."
During
"I will not interpret the card for you. I may ask you to separate fact from story, choose one next action, or define done for now."
Stop
"We are stopping the exercise here. A stop is valid data. Name one fact. Choose one ordinary next step. Put the deck away."
Close
"What was clear. What was confusing. What action did you choose. What should be simpler."
08
After your session

Complete the scoring form and return it to the tracker. Your feedback directly determines which cards get produced, revised, or retired. There are no wrong scores. Confusion is data.

Target: 30 tests across the first wave. Minimum 3 tests per card before any production decision. The deck is not public-ready until the data says so.

Confusion is not failure. It is revision signal.
